History of the Club

First set up in 1898, the club has had a long history. Its first captain A H Leech, a mining engineer, highlighted the clubs close connection with the town. Alongside him was the clubs first president Sir Francis Sharp Powell, a member of the Wigan parliment. The club is built on the grounds of Arley Hall, an abbey that dates back to the 12th century, some old parts still remain at the back of the now club house. However what is most memorable about the grounds is the moat and club house at the center. Rebuilt in 1767 the club house is what Wigan Golf Club is famous for, with its moat which houses the two balck swans and kingfishers.

If one was to ask a member at the club who the most famous member was, then the answer would be simple "John Wild". Captain from 1976-77 and Lancashire Union president in 1985 as well as President of the English Golf Union in the same year, John Wild is famous for his dedication towards Wigan and how he made it his goal to spread word about the club. One of his most famous achivements being that he was able to arrange Prince Andrew to visit the club."

The Surronding Area

Surronded by woodland, Wigan Golf Club not only has a stunning course but a stunning location. The surronding forest is an escape for hikers, naturists and animal lovers. For example there is a hiking trail that runs through the third. This will lead walkers onto a dirt path that you can follow which takes you around the grounds of Arley Hall and onto the grounds of the nearby Haigh Hall.

Not only does the area boast an appeal for the naturists it also attracts animal lovers. Located just outside the Club House is a 12th centuary moat, it has two famous residents, Arley and Marley, two black swans. However these rare birds are not the only animals you can find. The woodland is home to all sorts from the samll like; squirals and badgers to the large such as a herd of deer.

Change over the Years

If one is to mark the most notable change to the club since its opening it is that it has seen an expansion and some significant growth. In 2001, when the club was given a generous donation by Dave Whelan, of whome hole 11 is named after, which enabled the club to expand from just 9 holes to the now 18.
